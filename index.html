About Me

I'm a 2nd year PhD candidate in Engineering and Design at the University of Sussex. My research focus is in the field of Animal-Computer Interaction, specifically in applying techniques from HCI and animal cognition to better understand how animals percieve technology.

I hold a first class undergraduate degree in Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence with a specialisation in Neuroscience. My undergraduate thesis, which outlined my implementation of a system to improve the efficiency of behavioural coding, was also scored among the highest in the year. I now teach and mark for many of the modules I took during my undergraduate degree, and supervise final year students completing their own dissertations.

Sports

I'm also a former junior national team badminton player and I'm fortunate enough to have a sports scholarship for the duration of my studies at Sussex. While my professional sporting career ended as I entered university, I still spend most of my time out of the lab training, competing, or coaching the university team.

User Research

I'm experienced in a wide range of qualitative user research methodologies such as Participatory Design, A/B Testing, Rapid Iterative Prototyping, and Observational Ethnographic Field Studies. Using my knowledge of current methods in HCI, I've also developed new methods for user testing with animals.

Development

Throughout my undergradute degree, I've used C++, Java, Python, ReactJS and Matlab to create various apps, programs or AI's. In my PhD I tend to use Python (for natural language processing and data analysis) or Java (to create prototypes or basic applications used in my experimental studies)

Data Analysis

From thematic analysis of qualitative data, to statistical analysis of quantitative data, I've used a wide range of methods and tools to analyse data in my PhD. Most frequently, I use Python, SPSS, Nvivo and Qualtrics.

Design & Prototyping

I use paper prototypes, storyboards, UI wireframes, and heuristic evaluations to ensure the usability of my designs. I've also created functioning physical prototypes using Arduino and 3D printing.
